Antenna Integration Services

Building a wireless product but don't have an RF engineer on staff? You're not alone. Most hardware startups and small companies run into antenna problems late in development — poor range, failed compliance testing, or prototype performance that doesn't match the datasheet.

We help you get the antenna right the first time. Dr. Maloney brings 30+ years of antenna design experience, six US patents, and proprietary simulation tools to your project — without the overhead of a full-time hire.

How We Work With You

Our antenna integration service follows a proven process designed to minimize prototype iterations and get your product to market faster:

1. Requirements & Antenna Selection

We start by understanding your product: form factor, frequency bands, operating environment, regulatory targets, and performance goals. From there we recommend the right antenna approach — whether that's a commercial off-the-shelf component, a PCB trace antenna, or a custom design.

  • Link budget analysis to establish gain and sensitivity requirements
  • Antenna type trade study (chip, PCB, FPC, external, cavity-backed, etc.)
  • Vendor-neutral component recommendations with rationale
  • Ground plane and keep-out zone guidance for your PCB layout

2. Matching Network Design

The antenna is only as good as its match to the radio. We design the impedance matching network to maximize power transfer, minimize return loss, and maintain performance across your operating band.

  • Impedance matching network design (L, Pi, T topologies)
  • Component selection with standard values and vendor part numbers
  • Simulation-verified performance across the full operating band
  • PCB layout recommendations for the matching section

3. EM Simulation & Verification

Before you spin a board, we verify the antenna and matching network performance in simulation. This catches problems early — ground plane effects, nearby component coupling, enclosure detuning — when changes are cheap.

  • Full-wave electromagnetic simulation of antenna in your device context
  • Return loss (S11), radiation pattern, and efficiency predictions
  • Sensitivity analysis: what happens when the enclosure changes, or components shift
  • Clear go/no-go recommendation before prototype fabrication

4. Test & Tuning Plan

When your prototype arrives, you need to know exactly what to measure and how to fix what you find. We deliver a step-by-step test plan so your team (or test lab) can validate and tune the antenna with confidence.

  • VNA measurement procedure for return loss and impedance
  • Tuning guide: which matching components to adjust and in what order
  • Expected vs. acceptable performance criteria
  • Troubleshooting decision tree for common failure modes

5. Prototype Support

We don't disappear after delivering the report. When your prototype is on the bench, we're available to review your measurements, help interpret results, and guide tuning adjustments remotely.

  • Review of VNA measurements and comparison to simulation
  • Remote tuning guidance via video call
  • Matching network adjustments based on measured data
  • Support through to passing regulatory pre-scan
